Finance and Insurance - Design System Work

While at a sizeable financial organization, I was involved in three major different aspects of work on the Styleguide, a large living design system that contained the design assets of all brands owned by the company. These were imported into the brands' websites by developers, greatly reducing the amount of frontend development work needed to be done as elements were kept consistent and updated in the same place ready for re-use. 

Styleguide Maintenance
Frontend Development - LESS
I was involved with the updating of the styles in the design system to reflect changes to existing brands’ styling as well as the addition of new brands, translating designs into CSS components. Throughout my time there I updated many different elements, from icons to buttons, dropdown menus, accordions, colour palettes, and more. Every element had to be consistent with its brand, and even had to play nicely across brands in order to fit into the system. They all had to be responsive, as well as accessible. I worked in a small team reporting directly to the Head of Styleguide, who ensured that everything was upheld to a very high standard of usability.

Another designer helped us create a new logo and key colours for the Styleguide, which we fleshed out into components, layouts and styles. Icon Processing System Development
Frontend Development - Grunt tasks, Node JS, Javascript
I also worked on an icon processing system during my time at this organization. It required some modifications including removing the processing of jpgs/pngs and optimizing the processing of svgs. I modified the grunt tasks to take the colour palettes from each brand and generate svgs in each colour of each brand in the appropriate brand colours. It also took one arrow icon of each brand and rotated them into arrow-up, arrow-down, arrow-left and arrow-right, among other things like compressing and minimizing the icons.
